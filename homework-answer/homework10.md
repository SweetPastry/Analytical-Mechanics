## 第1题
We can easily write down the Hamiltonian of this system
$$
H=\frac{p^2}{2m}+c\mathrm{e}^{\gamma q},
$$
and transform it to Hamilton-Jacobi equation
$$
\frac{1}{2m}\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial q} \right) ^2+c\mathrm{e}^{\gamma q}+\frac{\partial S}{\partial t}=0.
$$
Note that there is no obvious $t$ in the expression of $H$ so let
$$
S=-Et+W\left( q, P \right),
$$
the $E$ is motion constant only depends on initial condition and we directly denote $P=E$, so $W\left(p, P\right)=W\left(p, P=E\right)=W\left( q \right)$. Substitude it in equation
$$
\frac{1}{2m}\left( \frac{\mathrm{d}W}{\mathrm{d}q} \right) ^2+c\mathrm{e}^{\gamma q}=E,
$$
solve it
$$
W=\pm \int{\sqrt{E-c\mathrm{e}^{\gamma q}}\mathrm{d}q}.
